## Design Context

### Users
Primary audiences are recruiters and general visitors evaluating Carson quickly. The interface should help them scan experience, projects, and case studies without friction, while still giving enough depth for a more careful read.

### Brand Personality
Clear, scannable, no-bloat. The voice should stay concise, technically credible, and calm. The interface should support relaxed exploration rather than pushing for attention with noisy visuals or dense copy.

### Aesthetic Direction
Preserve the site's existing editorial terminal/mono identity: strong typography, disciplined spacing, dark-first presentation today, and support for both light and dark themes in future work. Favor layouts that are easy to scan, visually quiet, and confidently minimal rather than decorative, trendy, or overly playful. There are no explicit reference sites, but future design work should avoid anything cluttered, flashy, or overloaded with UI chrome.

### Design Principles
1. Typography leads the design: prioritize legibility, hierarchy, line length, and rhythm before decorative treatment.
2. Optimize for scanning: make sections, headings, metadata, and calls to action easy to parse at a glance.
3. Remove bloat: every visual element and every sentence should earn its place.
4. Keep navigation calm: interactions should feel steady, deliberate, and low-friction rather than busy or high-arousal.
5. Build for dual themes: future UI decisions should translate cleanly to both dark and light modes without sacrificing readability.
